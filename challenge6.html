Network Security

Cybersecurity begins with securing your network perimeter. Firewalls filter incoming and outgoing traffic based on predetermined security rules.

Intrusion Detection Systems monitor network traffic for suspicious activity and known threats. IDS alerts security teams to potential breaches in real time.

Zero-trust architecture assumes no user or device is trusted by default. Every access request is verified regardless of location.

Data Protection

Encryption protects data at rest and in transit. AES-256 is the current standard for sensitive information across industries.

Regular backups are critical for ransomware recovery. Follow the 3-2-1 rule: three copies, two different media types, one offsite.

Access controls enforce least privilege. Users only get permissions needed for their role, reducing the impact of compromised credentials.
